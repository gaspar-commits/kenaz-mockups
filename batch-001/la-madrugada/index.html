The Story

Before it was a hotel,
it was a secret.

In the hills above Benissa, tucked between ancient pines and the shimmer of the Mediterranean, stands a villa that once belonged to Sarah Brightman -- the soprano whose voice defined a generation of musical theatre. This was where she came to disappear. No stage, no audience. Just light, stone, and the sound of cicadas.

In 2007, the villa began its second life. Carefully transformed into a boutique hotel with just eight rooms, every corner preserved the intimacy that made this place extraordinary. No grand lobbies. No crowds. Just the kind of quiet that money alone cannot buy.

Today, your host Pieter welcomes guests not as tourists, but as visitors to a private home. Breakfast on the terrace. Conversation by the pool. A recommendation whispered, not printed. This is hospitality as it was meant to be -- personal, unhurried, and deeply felt.

-- Pieter, Your Host
